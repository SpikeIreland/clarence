1. The Case for Modular Pricing

CLARENCE is not a single product used identically by every customer. It is a suite of distinct capabilities, each serving a different need. A real estate agent creating standard lettings agreements has no use for playbook compliance. A global bank running negotiation training exercises has no use for Quick Create. A mid-market outsourcer managing procurement contracts needs mediation and risk mitigation but may never touch training.

The previous pricing model — a flat per-seat fee for the "Pro" package — created two problems. First, it forced customers to pay for capabilities they would never use. Second, it constrained the seat count (5 seats) when some modules (like Protect) are company-wide infrastructure where limiting seats undermines the value proposition.

The modular model solves both problems: customers buy the capabilities they need, at the pricing unit that makes sense for each capability.


2. The Five Modules

CLARENCE's capabilities naturally cluster into five modules that map to distinct stages of the contract lifecycle and distinct buyer motivations.

    Create  →  Negotiate  →  Protect  →  Train  →  Discover
    "Draft"    "Mediate"    "Govern"    "Develop"  "Align"


3. Module Definitions

3.1 CREATE
Quick Create / QC Studio

What it does: The fastest path from "I need a contract" to "I have a negotiated agreement." Upload or select a template, CLARENCE auto-certifies clauses and maps ranges, and the user sets positions and invites a respondent. Negotiation is light-touch — ideal for standard agreements, renewals, and routine contracts.

Who it's for: Professionals who write contracts regularly but where each contract is relatively straightforward and does not require deep strategic analysis.

Example users:
- A real estate agent creating lettings or sales agreements
- A freelance consultant issuing standard service agreements
- A small business owner renewing supplier contracts
- An HR department producing employment agreements from templates

Core value proposition: Replaces the cycle of "download template → email back and forth → redline in Word → email again" with a structured, principled, AI-assisted process. Saves 3–5 hours per contract and eliminates adversarial email negotiation.

Pricing unit: Per company, per month. The studio is a workspace — limiting by seat count creates friction for small teams where multiple people may occasionally create contracts.

Includes:
- QC Studio access (unlimited users within the company)
- Template library access (standard templates by contract type)
- AI clause certification and range mapping
- Basic position-setting and respondent invitation
- Document Centre access for completed agreements

Does not include: Leverage analysis, strategic assessment, playbook compliance, training.

Suggested price: £99/month per company
Up to 10 active contract sessions. Additional sessions at £15 each.


3.2 NEGOTIATE
Contract Studio with Full Mediation

What it does: The full mediation pathway. Both parties complete strategic assessments. CLARENCE calculates leverage, generates three-position analysis (initiator, respondent, Clarence recommendation), and provides structured clause-by-clause negotiation with trade-off detection, party chat, and AI-assisted guidance. The Contract Studio is the platform's core differentiator — the space where The Honest Broker operates.

Who it's for: Legal and procurement professionals managing contracts where the negotiation itself is strategically important — where positions need analysis, leverage needs to be understood, and the quality of the agreement matters as much as its speed.

Example users:
- A procurement team at Lloyds negotiating ISDA master agreements
- Ventrica negotiating outsourcing contracts as a service provider
- CapGemini structuring alliance or partnership agreements
- A mid-market company negotiating a significant SaaS vendor contract

Core value proposition: Replaces weeks of redlining, legal review, and adversarial back-and-forth with structured, transparent, data-driven mediation. Reduces legal fees (typically £2,000–10,000 per negotiation), accelerates time-to-signature, and produces better agreements because both parties understand the trade-offs.

Pricing unit: Per user, per month. Each negotiator needs their own view of positions, leverage, and chat. The per-user model scales naturally with team size.

Includes:
- Everything in Create
- Contract Studio access with full mediation
- Leverage calculation and three-position analysis
- Trade-off detection and creative compromise suggestions
- Party chat (structured dialogue between parties)
- CLARENCE AI chat with clause-specific context
- Negotiation history and position tracking
- Solo prep state → active negotiation transition
- Clause-specific feedback reporting

Does not include: Playbook compliance, cross-checking, training.

Suggested price: £249/user/month
Unlimited active contract sessions. Up to 5 respondents per contract.


3.3 PROTECT
Playbook Lifecycle — Build, Verify, Cross-Check, Enforce

What it does: The risk mitigation suite. A company codifies its negotiation boundaries — ideal positions, acceptable minimums, deal-breakers, escalation paths — into a playbook. CLARENCE then enforces those boundaries automatically: cross-checking templates before negotiation begins, monitoring compliance during negotiation in real time, and alerting when red lines are approached or breached.

Who it's for: Organisations that want consistent, governed negotiation outcomes across their team — where individual negotiators should operate within defined boundaries rather than making ad-hoc judgement calls on every clause.

Example users:
- Lloyds ensuring all procurement contracts meet internal risk thresholds
- A mid-market company with 3 contract negotiators who need consistent positions
- CapGemini defining global negotiation standards for alliance agreements
- Any organisation that has (or should have) a negotiation policy but lacks the tools to enforce it

Core value proposition: Democratises enterprise-grade contract governance. Previously, only organisations with dedicated legal teams and established processes could enforce negotiation boundaries. CLARENCE makes this accessible to any company with a negotiation policy — or helps them create one.

Pricing unit: Per company, per month. Playbooks are company-wide infrastructure. Every negotiator in the company should be protected, not just those with a "Protect seat." Limiting seats here undermines the core value proposition — governance only works when it covers everyone.

Includes:
- Playbook creation (from uploaded documents with AI extraction or from scratch with guided templates)
- Two-pass AI extraction with source quote anchoring and quality validation
- Verification dashboard with rule editing
- Pre-negotiation cross-check (score any template against the active playbook)
- Real-time compliance monitoring during negotiation
- Red line breach alerts and escalation triggers
- Compliance guidance banners in the Contract Studio
- Unlimited users within the company

Does not include: Contract Studio access (requires Negotiate module), training.

Suggested price: £349/month per company
One active playbook included. Additional playbooks (e.g., per contract type) at £99/month each.

Note: Protect requires at least one user with a Negotiate subscription to be useful, since compliance monitoring operates within the Contract Studio. Protect without Negotiate provides cross-check only (pre-negotiation risk assessment).


3.4 TRAIN
Training Studio with Dynamic AI Opponents

What it does: Risk-free negotiation practice against AI opponents that are dynamically generated for each session. CLARENCE assesses the trainee, designs a realistic scenario targeting their weaknesses, generates a unique opponent with personality and objectives calibrated to the deal context, and provides a comprehensive post-session debrief with scoring and skill progression.

Who it's for: Legal and commercial professionals who want to improve their negotiation skills, and the organisations that want to develop their teams' capabilities systematically.

Example users:
- Goldman Sachs training junior negotiators on ISDA agreements
- A law firm running negotiation exercises for trainees
- A corporate legal team preparing for a high-stakes negotiation
- A university incorporating principled negotiation into its curriculum
- An individual lawyer building their negotiation skillset

Core value proposition: Replaces expensive, logistics-heavy training programs (typically £500–2,000 per day per participant) with on-demand, personalised, AI-driven practice. Each session is unique — different opponent, different scenario, different pressure points. The debrief system creates a closed-loop learning cycle where every session improves the next. No scheduling, no role-play partners, no facilitator needed.

Pricing unit: Per trainee, per month. Training is an individual development investment. Organisations purchase seats for the team members they want to develop.

Includes:
- Training Studio access with unlimited sessions
- Dynamic persona generation (unique AI opponent per session)
- Scenario design tailored to individual weaknesses
- Live AI opponent behaviour (counter-moves, in-character chat)
- Post-session scorecard (A–D grading, position strength, agreement rate)
- Comprehensive debrief with teachable moments
- Cumulative skill tracking and experience progression
- Playbook-based training (when combined with Protect)

Future additions (see Chapter 11.5 of the Bible):
- Leaderboards and team rankings
- Achievement badges and mastery levels
- Championship and competition formats
- Category mastery progression

Does not include: Live contract negotiation (requires Create or Negotiate module).

Suggested price: £149/trainee/month
Unlimited training sessions. Minimum 3 trainees for team accounts.

Volume pricing:
- 10–24 trainees: £129/trainee/month
- 25–49 trainees: £109/trainee/month
- 50+ trainees: £89/trainee/month


3.5 DISCOVER (Planned)
Tendering & Principled Provider Alignment

What it does: Before any contract exists, Discover enables an organisation to identify which providers are most aligned with their negotiation principles. By comparing playbooks, CLARENCE scores compatibility — surfacing where positions naturally converge and where negotiation will be required — before a single clause is written.

Who it's for: Procurement teams managing supplier selection, organisations running formal tendering processes, and any company that wants to reduce the risk of entering negotiations that are doomed to fail.

Example users:
- A local authority selecting from multiple outsourcing providers
- A financial institution evaluating technology vendors
- A corporate legal team assessing which potential partners share their commercial values

Core value proposition: Inverts the procurement sequence. Instead of discovering misalignment after weeks of negotiation, Discover surfaces alignment and friction points before the contract conversation begins. This saves time, reduces failed negotiations, and ensures that when negotiation does begin, both parties know where they stand.

Pricing unit: Per company, per month. Tendering is a procurement function, not an individual activity.

Includes:
- Playbook-to-playbook compatibility scoring
- Provider alignment ranking
- Gap analysis before negotiation begins
- Integration with Protect module for seamless transition to negotiation

Suggested price: To be determined (module not yet built).
Initial thinking: £299/month per company or per-tender pricing.

8. Competitive Context

CLARENCE does not have a direct competitor — no existing platform combines neutral mediation, risk mitigation, and training in a single environment. The closest comparisons are:

Contract Lifecycle Management (Ironclad, Juro, DocuSign CLM): £40–150/user/month. These serve one party (the contract owner), not both. They optimise for speed and storage, not negotiation quality.

Legal Research & Compliance (Practical Law, LexisNexis): £100–400/user/month. These provide information, not active mediation. They help lawyers know what's standard — CLARENCE helps both parties reach agreement on what's fair.

Negotiation Training (Scotwork, The Gap Partnership): £1,000–3,000/participant/day. In-person, event-based, logistics-heavy. CLARENCE's Training Studio provides unlimited, personalised, on-demand practice at a fraction of the cost.

CLARENCE's pricing should reflect its unique positioning: more valuable than a CLM tool (it improves outcomes, not just workflow), more accessible than premium training (on-demand vs. event-based), and more comprehensive than any single-category competitor.


9. Open Questions for Team Discussion

9.1 Per-user vs per-company: Is the distinction clear enough? Should Negotiate also be per-company for smaller teams?

9.2 Protect standalone value: Can Protect be sold without Negotiate? The cross-check feature works independently, but real-time compliance requires the Contract Studio. Should we offer a "Cross-Check Only" tier?

9.3 Training volume pricing: The volume discounts are aggressive (£149 → £89 at 50+). Are these sustainable? What's the marginal cost of additional training sessions?

9.4 Annual contracts: Should we offer annual pricing with a discount (e.g., 2 months free = ~17% discount)? This improves cash flow predictability.

9.5 Fair use limits: Should any module have explicit usage caps (e.g., AI interactions per month), or should all modules be unlimited within their scope?

9.6 Provider pricing: When a company invites providers to negotiate, should the provider need their own subscription? Currently providers are session-scoped guests. This is a key competitive advantage — the initiator pays, the respondent participates for free.

9.7 Discover pricing model: Per-company monthly subscription, or per-tender pricing (e.g., £99 per tender process)? Per-tender may better match procurement budgets.

9.8 Enterprise custom pricing: At what point does custom pricing kick in? By user count (e.g., 20+ users), by module combination, or by revenue value?
