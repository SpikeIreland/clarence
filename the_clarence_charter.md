THE CLARENCE CHARTER
An internal declaration of who we are, why we exist, and how we choose to act - so that everything we build, say, and invite others into is coherent, principled, and deliberate.
 
1. Why This Charter Exists
Clarence was not founded to be merely another piece of software in the legal-tech ecosystem. It was founded because something felt fundamentally misaligned in the way agreements - the very foundations of business relationships - are currently made.
Negotiation today is too often:
•	Adversarial where it could be collaborative
•	Opaque where it could be transparent
•	Slow where it could be decisive
•	Expensive where it could be efficient
•	Ego-driven where it should be principled
Most negotiations begin with an unspoken truth: both parties already know roughly where they will land. Yet the ritual requires posturing, concealment, tactical brinkmanship, and drawn-out red-lining - all of which erode trust before a relationship has even begun.
Clarence exists to challenge that ritual.
This charter is a shared reference point - a quiet but firm alignment between us as founders - so that as we grow, partner, sell, design, and inevitably face resistance, we remember who we are being, not just what we are building.
 
2. Our Identity: Clarence, The Honest Broker
Clarence is a broker - but not of deals. Of clarity.
We are not an advocate for one side or the other. We do not “win” negotiations on behalf of a party. We do not amplify leverage as a weapon.
Instead, Clarence occupies a rare and deliberate position:
A neutral, principled intermediary that exists to serve the agreement itself.
Like a good judge, mediator, or referee, Clarence is trusted not because it is powerful, but because it is fair. Not because it persuades, but because it reveals.
Our identity rests on three pillars:
1.	Neutrality - We serve both parties equally, without hidden incentives.
2.	Transparency - We surface leverage, trade-offs, and constraints explicitly.
3.	Principle - We privilege durable, relationship-preserving outcomes over tactical advantage.
If Clarence were human, it would be the calm presence in the room when voices rise - the one that says, “Let’s slow this down and look at what actually matters.” Perhaps like Clarence Darrow, after whom we’ve named the tool!
 
3. Our Purpose: To Change How Agreements Are Made
Our purpose is not simply to make negotiation faster - though it will. Not simply to make it cheaper - though it should. Not simply to reduce stress - though it does.
Our deeper purpose is this:
To fundamentally improve the quality of agreements by improving the quality of the negotiation environment in which they are formed.
We believe:
•	Better environments produce better decisions
•	Better decisions produce better agreements
•	Better agreements produce better relationships
Clarence exists to create an environment where:
•	Truth is safer to disclose
•	Creativity is rewarded
•	Trade-offs are explicit rather than implicit
•	Outcomes feel fair even when compromise is required
When Clarence works well, both parties should be able to say:
“This agreement reflects reality - not theatre.”
 
4. Our View of the Problem (and Why It Persists)
Traditional negotiation persists not because it is good - but because it is familiar.
Its inefficiencies are masked by:
•	Professional habit
•	Institutional inertia
•	Billable-hour economics
•	Fear of disclosure
•	Human ego and identity attachment
The system rewards:
•	Withholding information
•	Overplaying leverage
•	Framing compromise as weakness
Yet paradoxically, most experienced negotiators know this is suboptimal. All as described to us by John!
Clarence does not assume people are irrational. It assumes they are trapped in a structure that makes rational, principled behaviour difficult.
Our work is not to change people. It is to change the structure around them.
 
5. Our Values (and How They Show Up in What We Build)
5.1 Principle Over Position
We believe durable agreements are grounded in principles, not postures.
In practice:
•	We design processes that surface interests, not just demands
•	We prioritise long-term relationship health in our recommendations
•	We resist features that encourage manipulation or gamesmanship
 
5.2 Transparency Creates Trust
Opacity feels safe in the short term - but it corrodes outcomes.
In practice:
•	Clarence makes leverage visible, not mystified
•	Trade-offs are framed explicitly rather than buried in legal language
•	We explain why a suggestion exists, not just what it is
 

5.3 Speed Without Recklessness
Slow negotiation is not careful negotiation.
In practice:
•	We structure conversations so decisions happen earlier
•	We reduce unnecessary iteration
•	We value decisiveness - but never at the cost of understanding
 
5.4 Respect for Human Dignity
Negotiations fail when people feel diminished, cornered, or unseen.
In practice:
•	Clarence removes ego battles from the equation
•	The system speaks calmly, neutrally, and without judgment
•	We design for psychological safety, not pressure
 
5.5 Creativity Over Zero-Sum Thinking
The best agreements often look obvious only after someone reframes the problem.
In practice:
•	Clarence suggests options humans might not see
•	We encourage value-creation before value-division
•	We design for possibility, not just compromise
 
6. Clarence as a Cultural Statement
We are not just introducing a tool. We are proposing a different way of working.
Clarence quietly asserts that:
•	Professionalism does not require hostility
•	Fairness is not naïveté
•	Transparency is not weakness
•	AI can elevate human judgment rather than replace it
This will create resistance.
Some will distrust neutrality. Some will miss the theatre. Some will fear what becomes visible.
That resistance is not a sign of failure. It is a signal that something foundational is being questioned.
 
7. How This Charter Guides Our Decisions
When faced with ambiguity, we return to simple questions:
•	Does this strengthen or weaken trust?
•	Does this clarify reality or obscure it?
•	Does this help both parties reach a better agreement?
•	Does this align with being The Honest Broker?
If the answer is unclear, we pause. If the answer is no, we decline - even if it is expedient.
 
8. A Note to Ourselves (and to Future Partners)
Clarence will evolve. The product will change. The market will respond in unexpected ways.
This charter is not a constraint. It is a compass.
If we remain aligned to it, Clarence will not just succeed commercially - it will matter.
And if, one day, negotiation itself feels calmer, clearer, and more humane because of what we built here - then we will know we aimed at the right thing.
